## [CC-01] Incident trigger

Treat an alarm, missing temperature record, damaged cold-chain package, or temperature outside the approved product range as an incident. Record detection time, device, location, product identifiers, quantities, and observed temperature range.

## [CC-02] Immediate quarantine

Stop sale and movement of affected stock, label it QUARANTINE—DO NOT USE, and place it in a secure temperature-controlled location where possible. Do not return stock to sale based on appearance or an AI-generated assessment.

## [CC-03] Escalation

Notify the pharmacist in charge and cold-chain lead immediately. Escalation is required for any confirmed excursion, repeated alarm, missing evidence, or affected product that has already been supplied. Supplier contact follows approved quality channels.

## [CC-04] Disposition boundary

Only an authorised quality or clinical professional may approve release, return, or destruction. The AI system must refuse requests for medical suitability, dosing, patient risk, or whether an exposed medicine is safe to use.

## [CC-05] Evidence and closure

Retain temperature logs, calibration status, photographs where approved, affected quantities, quarantine location, decision owner, disposition, inventory adjustment, and closure timestamp. Close only after authorised disposition and stock reconciliation.

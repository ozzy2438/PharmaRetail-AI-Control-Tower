## [SE-01] Purpose and authorised users

This restricted SOP governs supplier delivery escalation. National supply-chain analysts, authorised AI application services, and administrators may access supplier escalation details. Store managers must route requests through their area manager and must not receive restricted supplier contact or commercial terms.

## [SE-02] Trigger conditions

Open an escalation when actual delivery is later than the expected delivery date, delivered quantity is below the ordered quantity, OTIF equals zero, or a delay creates a projected stockout within three days. Consolidate repeated failures for the same supplier and product.

## [SE-03] Severity and response

Classify critical when a recall replacement, cold-chain product, or national stockout is affected; high when more than five stores are affected; otherwise classify standard. Critical cases require supply-chain duty manager acknowledgement within two hours. High cases require acknowledgement within one business day.

## [SE-04] Supplier communication

Provide order identifier, product, ordered and delivered quantities, expected and actual dates, affected regions, and requested recovery date. Do not disclose customer, employee, authentication, pricing-contract, or unrelated supplier information.

## [SE-05] Closure and performance evidence

Close only when a recovery delivery is confirmed, an approved alternative source is allocated, or the business accepts the shortage. Record resolution, responsible owner, promised recovery date, OTIF impact, and linked incident identifiers.

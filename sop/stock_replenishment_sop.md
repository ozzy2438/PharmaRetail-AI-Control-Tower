## [SR-01] Purpose and scope

This SOP governs replenishment of ranged retail products after a stockout alert, low days-of-cover signal, or failed shelf replenishment. It applies to store, area, and national supply-chain operations. Prescription decisions and clinical substitutions are outside scope.

## [SR-02] Stockout verification

The store manager must confirm the product identifier, shelf quantity, back-room quantity, pending receipts, and inventory adjustment history before escalation. A system stockout is confirmed only when closing stock is zero and expected demand is greater than zero. Record discrepancies before changing the replenishment order.

## [SR-03] Replenishment response

For a confirmed stockout, check the next scheduled delivery and create a replenishment request for the approved order multiple. Where a delivery is already due within one business day, avoid a duplicate order and monitor receipt. Do not transfer recalled, quarantined, damaged, or temperature-excursion stock.

## [SR-04] Escalation thresholds

Escalate to the area manager when expected demand cannot be covered within two business days, the same product stocks out twice in seven days, or the replenishment request fails. Escalate supplier-related delays under the Supplier Escalation SOP.

## [SR-05] Evidence and closure

Retain the stock snapshot, replenishment request identifier, expected receipt date, action owner, and closure timestamp. Close the event only after usable stock is received or an approved range decision is recorded.
